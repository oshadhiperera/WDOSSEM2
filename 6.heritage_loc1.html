TEMPLE OF TOOTH - KANDY

The Temple of the Tooth, also known as the Dalada Maligawa, is one of Kandy's most famous landmarks. It lies in the centre of Kandy town, bordered by the business sector and the lake. The Maligawa used to be the royal palace of the monarch and his consorts, but after the Kandyan Kingdom fell apart, the palace was converted into a Buddhist shrine housing Buddha's tooth. It is a must-see destination for all visitors.

Visitors can see Buddhist rites, visit the museum, which has various old items, and pay their respects to the tooth relic at one of the Buddhist ceremonies performed on the site. The architectural splendor and historical significance of the Dalada Maligawa will astound you. The World Buddhism Museum, the Aluth Maligawa, and the Audience Hall are all worth seeing. Keep in mind that the Maligawa is a Buddhist shrine, therefore you should wear clothing that covers your shoulders and legs.
