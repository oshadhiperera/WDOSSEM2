YALA NATIONAL PARK

Yala is the second biggest national park in Sri Lanka. It's at the south-east corner of the island, with the Indian Ocean to the south. Thousands of visitors visit the park each year from all around the world in the hopes of viewing and photographing rare animal encounters. Yala has a wide range of environments. Dense shrub woodlands, thorny forest thickets, vast meadows, and gorgeous wetlands are among them. Each of these locations is home to a diverse range of species. The majestic Asian elephant, the fascinating sloth bear, and the deadly mugger crocodile are just a few of the well-known species. Yala is also one of the greatest spots to see leopards in the world. Leopards have a large population.
