ARTS AND CRAFTS

As a country which is full of culture, Sri Lanka has a very unique and a long history of arts and crafts. Mainly there is,
Masks
In the mask industry the most popular act is the 18 Sanni. Where they carve 18 different masks to represent the devils known as Yakkas. And then a devil dance is performed by wearing them. Ambalangoda is the mostly known place for carving masks.
Lacquer work (Laksha)
Lacquerware is made by using a wax derived from a species of insects, which is now imported from India.
They make colorful and bright things like,
Walking sticks, Handles of flags, Hand-fans, Bowls, Vases, Containers, Decorative items etc.
Dumbara waves
Dumbara weaves was an art which could only be found as decorative items and mats. But now the art is shown with all kinds of beautiful designs in bags and purses, rugs, table mats and carpets. Thalagune Uda Dumbara, still have dumbara wavers from the Kandyan era who holds the secrets to the skill.

TEA INDUSTRY

Tea production is one of Sri Lanka's most important sources of foreign currency, contributing for 2% of GDP and providing roughly US$1.3 billion in 2021. In 1995, tea plantations and estates directly employed 215,338 people. In addition, smallholder tea farming employs thousands of people and is the major source of income for tens of thousands of households. Sri Lanka is the world's fourth largest tea producer. Sri Lanka was the world's largest tea exporter (rather than producer) in 1995, accounting for 23% of worldwide exports, and it ranked second in tea export income behind China in 2020.

SPICES

Sri Lanka is noted for its savory genuine cuisine, thanks to a major supply of indigenous spices and the supposed antiquity of the spice trade with its core features all over the world. Ceylon cuisine is a must-visit destination for anybody who enjoys a wide range of flavors and aromas. Ceylon spices have a sweet, delectable aroma and lend the perfect texture, color, and punch to the majority of their distinctive dishes. Some are,
Cinnamon
Black pepper
