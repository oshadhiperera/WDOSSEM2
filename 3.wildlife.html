EXPLORE THE WILD

MINNERIYA NATIONAL PARK

Minneriya national park is located in the dry zone of north central province Sri Lanka. The park is known for the world famous Great Elephant Gathering of the Asian wild elephants. There are 350 elephants seen in 8,890 hectare park. The best period to visit the park is in May to October.

BUNDALA NATIONAL PARK

Bundala National Park is a lovely maze of canals, lagoons, and dunes that gleam like gold in the evening sun. Thousands of colorful birds make this paradise home, ranging in size from small bee-eaters to huge open-billed stalks. The Ramsar Convention on Wetlands has designated it as an important wetland refuge. There are also numerous huge animals, including 15 to 60 elephants, depending on the season (December is the best month).

YALA NATIONAL PARK

Yala is the second biggest national park in Sri Lanka. It's at the south-east corner of the island, with the Indian Ocean to the south. Thousands of visitors visit the park each year from all around the world in the hopes of viewing and photographing rare animal encounters. Yala has a wide range of environments. Dense shrub woodlands, thorny forest thickets, vast meadows, and gorgeous wetlands are among them. Each of these locations is home to a diverse range of species. The majestic Asian elephant, the fascinating sloth bear, and the deadly mugger crocodile are just a few of the well-known species. Yala is also one of the greatest spots to see leopards in the world. Leopards have a large population.
